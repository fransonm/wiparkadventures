Park Description

Brunet Island State Park is located north of Chippewa Falls on and around the Chippewa River. On the island there are hiking trails, a boat landing, beach, shelter, playground, and ball field. The section of the park off the island along the river has a hiking/skiing trail.

I spent all of my time at the park on the island. The Pine and Timber trails go through the woods in the middle of the island connecting the boat landing, beach/shelter, and camping areas. My wildlife sightings incuded deer, squirrels, and chipmunks. On my way back to the parking lot, there was a small buck standing in the trail.

The Jean Brunet Nature Trail is a loop at the north end of the island. It follows along the shore offering beautiful views of the river and fall colors.

Leaving from the boat landing, I followed the Spruce Trail along the shoreline. The trail took me to the ball field (a grass field with a backstop and bench), playground, and eventually back to the beach.
