Park Description

Amnicon Falls State Park is in the northwest corner of Wisconsin on the Amnicon River. The park features waterfalls, hiking trails, and a snowshoeing trail.

The more popular trails run along the river, offering views of the four named waterfalls: Upper Falls, Lower Falls, Snake Pit Falls, and Now and Then Falls. The Upper Falls is on the section of the river above the covered walking bridge that allows access to the island trail from the main parking lot. Walking up river from the covered bridge, you will find nice views of the falls as well as the bridge from above. The Lower Falls is below the covered bridge. To get the best view of the falls with the bridge, follow the trail from the parking lot around the bend in the river. Snake Pit Falls can be found by following the trail that loops around the island.

Now and Then Falls is at the end of the short trail off the end of the main parking lot. When I visted the park, this falls was all dried up.

If you take the uncovered bridge off the island, the trail will lead you along the river to a play area and reservable shelter. Following the road further into the park will take you to the head of the Thimbleberry Trail and the snowshoe trail. The Thimbleberry Trail is a loop trail that runs along the river, then turns away from the river into the woods. A trail spurs off of the Thimbleberry and dead-ends at the Sandstone Quarry, which is now a pond. When I hiked the trail, the falls colors reflected beautifully on the pond.
